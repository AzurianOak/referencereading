What Is It Like to Be a Bat?

Thomas Nagel · 1974 · The Philosophical Review

Category: Philosophy of Mind · Cited by 2,346 papers

★★★★☆

Average Rating

Abstract

In this influential essay, Thomas Nagel argues that conscious experience has a subjective character that is inherently inaccessible to objective, physical explanations. Using the example of bats, Nagel examines the limits of reductionist approaches to the mind, emphasizing that there is something it is like to be a conscious organism.
